I am currently an Assistant Professor of Economics and Wieler Family Assistant Professor at [Boston College](https://www.bc.edu/bc-web/schools/mcas/departments/economics.html) in Chestnut Hill, MA. My research interests are in industrial organization and antitrust. In particular, I study how automobile retail markets are organized and the implications of regulations and market power of dealers and manufacturers. I also have interests in issues of market power and antitrust issues in airline markets.


### Current Research In Progress

"[Market Structure and Competition in Airline Markets](http://charliemurry.github.io/files/CMT_R&R_JPE_Final.pdf)," with F. Ciliberto and E. Tamer.

**Summary:** *We develop a methodology to estimate static games of entry and pricing and quantify the price and product repositioning effects of an airline merger.*

"[Price Discrimination in International Airline Markets](http://charliemurry.github.io/files/AMW_2018-11-23.pdf)," with Gaurab Aryal and Jonathan W. Williams.

**Summary:** *Using a model of dynamic pricing and consumer screening, we estimate that incomplete information in airline pricing leads to a 20% gap between current welfare and first-best welfare.*

"​The Evolution of Market Power in the US Auto Industry," with Paul Grieco and Ali Yurukoglu. [Download Slides](http://charliemurry.github.io/files/carMarkups_20min.pdf)

**Summary:** *Markups in the Auto Industry gradually decreased over the last 40 years.*

"[Local Effects of Franchise Contract Regulations](http://charliemurry.github.io/files/termination_laws_JLE.pdf)," with Peter Newberry

**Summary:** *States that have laws that restrict franchisors from terminating franchisee contracts have fewer franchises.*

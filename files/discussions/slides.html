Overview

Predation

  1. Sacrifice short-run profits through intensified competition in order to eliminate competitors
  2. Gain future profits.

Standard Test -- "Areeda-Turner" rule
Are prices below a measure of (short-run) marginal cost?

Why is this tricky?

  1. False positives | low prices are good!
  2. Inherently dynamic concept.
  3. Correlated unobservables.

Key Idea

After entry, it is profitable to decrease capacity because capacities are strategic substitutes.

My hand needs to be held through the theory more...

  1. This is true for quantities, is it also true for capacities?
  2. Actually, this is not always true for quantities.
    • see Bulow et. al. (1985) dominant firm argument.
    • Short-run profitable for dominant firm to hold excess capacity.
  3. My reading of Bulow is actually entry deterrence (more later).

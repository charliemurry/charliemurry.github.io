Suggestion 3: The Value of Media Buyers

Search

  • Clearly important for intermediaries.

Consulting services

  • This is just preference for media buyers.

Quantity Discounts

  • But advertisers could go direct...
  • This seems like saying: "Media buyers provide value because they charge a lower price than infinity."
  • This is more like a service to the billboard company.

"Value" and "welfare" are used without a clear definition until late in the paper.

 \,
The authors are not estimating the fixed costs of intermediaries.

 \,
What is really being measured is:

  1. Consumer surplus and
  2. market outcomes.

Suggestion 4: Eliminate Intermediaries

 \,
If we want to know the value of intermediaries, why not simulate their elimination?

 \,
I think you have the model to do this, including simulating eqm. price changes.

Suggestion 5: Bargaining

  1. This industry looks different in the US.
    • Media buyers attract clients (long term relationships) and then purchase spots from content providers.
  2. There is a fixed capacity.
    • I don't see this anywhere in the model.
  3. Do you really need the bargaining game?
    • Recover total marginal costs using "standard" methods.
    • None of the "value" you discuss is related to share of rents between media buyers and billboard content providers.
    • I think you could still do the "eliminate" counterfactual.

Conclusion

This is a nice paper and the authors are pushing our knowledge of intermediaries.

 \,
The question is an important unanswered question in a lot of related literature.

 \,
Some re-writing could help give the paper bigger impact.

 \,
More substantial issues they could think about now or in their next project.

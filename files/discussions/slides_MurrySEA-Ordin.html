"Investment and Taxation: the Case of Oil and Gas in the Permian Basin,"

by Andrey Ordin (Duke JMC)


Charlie Murry | Boston College


SEA 2019, Ft. Lauderdale

What is the Story?

Local and state govts want drilling tax revenue.

Govts raise revenue through initial auction and revenue sharing.

  • What is the right thing to do?
  • At what level?

If taxes are too high → discourage investment/drilling.

  • Revenue-based royalties are distortionary.

How should oil proceeds be taxed?

"Laffer Curve" for drilling.

Tax rates can be too high ⇢ lower tax revenues.

Except there is a third important axis.

  • Drilling activity is important per se.

 \,

Why is this hard?

Typical auction model does not incorporate post-auction actions, like investment.

Take Away

Simple alternatives do not improve much on status quo.

Profit tax helps a lot.

Questions/Comments I

This looks reasonable, but I am worried the model over-predicts responses to price.

There could be a lot happening in the years after the auction and before drilling.

  • Learning story of Hodgson or Covert and Sweeney

Questions/Comments II

The focus on taxes is more narrow than it could be.

The same economics applies to any type of royalty payments.

  • Drilling, see Herrnstadt, Kellogg, and Lewis (wp).
  • Large literature on franchising.
    • No empirical evidence?
